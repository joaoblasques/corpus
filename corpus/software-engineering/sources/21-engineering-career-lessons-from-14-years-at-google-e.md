# 21 Engineering Career Lessons from 14 Years at Google

> **Quick intake** (obsidian). raw stub: `notes-03-resources-articles-engineering-career-lessons-14-years-google.md`

The document shares 21 engineering career lessons from 14 years at Google, emphasizing the importance of navigating people, politics, alignment, and ambiguity to create value. It highlights the need to balance technical mastery with human skills. The key takeaway is that engineers who thrive are those who understand the human side of engineering.

**Key topics**
- User obsession over technology obsession
- Bias towards action
- Clarity is seniority; cleverness is overhead
- Novelty is a loan repaid in outages
- Your code doesn't advocate for you; people do
- The best code is code you never had to write
