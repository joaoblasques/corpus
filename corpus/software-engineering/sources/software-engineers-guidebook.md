# Source: The Software Engineer's Guidebook (Gergely Orosz)

**TL;DR**: A 359-page career reference by Gergely Orosz (The Pragmatic Engineer), covering the full arc from new developer through senior, tech lead, and staff/principal engineer at Big Tech and scaleups. Focus on both "soft" skills (ownership, collaboration, stakeholder management) and "hard" skills (coding, testing, architecture, reliability). Written as a reference to return to as seniority grows — not a read-once book [^src1].

## Book structure

Six standalone parts [^src1]:

| Part | Level | Topics |
|---|---|---|
| I | All | Career paths, owning career, performance reviews, promotions, switching jobs |
| II | Competent developer | Getting things done, coding, software development, productive tooling |
| III | Well-rounded senior | Collaboration, software engineering, testing, software architecture |
| IV | Pragmatic tech lead | Project management, shipping to production, stakeholder management, team structure |
| V | Staff/principal | Business understanding, collaboration, reliable software systems, software architecture |
| VI | Conclusion | Lifelong learning, further reading |

## Key framing

"Naming and levels vary, but the principles of what makes a great engineer who is impactful at the individual, team, and organizational levels, are remarkably constant." [^src1]

The author's career arc — developer at agencies → JP Morgan → Microsoft → engineering manager at Uber → author — grounds the advice in lived experience at multiple tiers. The preface acknowledges that "it's not always those who work hardest or deliver the highest quality work who are awarded the biggest bonuses and win prized promotions" [^src1].

**Context for leveling**: the book aligns to Big Tech expectations (Google L5, Meta E5 "senior"), which are higher than at lower-tier companies. Senior at a startup ≠ senior at Google [^src1].

## Scope limitations

Explicitly excludes timely topics (job market conditions, funding levels, emerging tech) — those live in the companion Pragmatic Engineer Newsletter (Substack's #1 tech newsletter, Bloomberg-recognized) [^src1].
