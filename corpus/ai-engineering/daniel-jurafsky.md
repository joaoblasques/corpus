# Daniel Jurafsky

TL;DR: Computational linguistics professor at Stanford University; co-author (with James H. Martin) of *Speech and Language Processing*, the standard NLP/speech textbook now in a 3rd edition draft dated January 6, 2026.

## Affiliation

- Stanford University (listed on the 3rd edition title page). [^slp-p01]

## *Speech and Language Processing* (3rd edition draft)

Co-authored with [James H. Martin](/ai-engineering/james-martin.md) (University of Colorado at Boulder). The draft is dated January 6, 2026, and spans 626 pages across 25 chapters plus bibliography and subject index. [^slp-p01]

The book is divided into two parts: [^slp-p01]

**Part I — Large Language Models** (chapters 1–16): Introduction, Words and Tokens, N-gram Language Models, Logistic Regression, Embeddings, Neural Networks, Large Language Models, Transformers, Masked Language Models, Post-training (instruction tuning, alignment, test-time compute), Retrieval-based Models, Machine Translation, RNNs and LSTMs, Phonetics and Speech Feature Extraction, Automatic Speech Recognition, Text-to-Speech.

**Part II — Annotating Linguistic Structure** (chapters 17–25): Sequence Labeling for POS and Named Entities, Context-Free Grammars and Constituency Parsing, Dependency Parsing, Information Extraction (relations, events, time), Semantic Role Labeling, Lexicons for Sentiment/Affect/Connotation, Coreference Resolution and Entity Linking, Discourse Coherence, Conversation and its Structure.

The preface invites reader feedback: "Comments and typos welcome!" [^slp-p01]
