# James H. Martin

TL;DR: Computer science professor at the University of Colorado at Boulder; co-author (with Daniel Jurafsky) of *Speech and Language Processing*, the standard NLP/speech textbook, now in its 3rd edition draft (January 2026).

## Affiliation

Martin is affiliated with the University of Colorado at Boulder. [^slp-p01]

## Key contributions

***Speech and Language Processing: An Introduction to Natural Language Processing, Computational Linguistics, and Speech Recognition with Language Models*** (co-authored with Daniel Jurafsky of Stanford University) is the primary work cited in this corpus. [^slp-p01]

The 3rd edition draft (dated January 6, 2026) spans 38 chapters across two parts: [^slp-p01]

- **Part I — Large Language Models** (chapters 1–16): covers tokenization, n-gram language models, logistic regression, embeddings, neural networks, LLMs, transformers, masked language models, post-training (instruction tuning, alignment, test-time compute), retrieval-based models, machine translation, RNNs/LSTMs, phonetics and speech feature extraction, automatic speech recognition, and text-to-speech.
- **Part II — Annotating Linguistic Structure** (chapters 17–25): covers sequence labeling (POS tagging, NER), context-free grammars and constituency parsing, dependency parsing, information extraction (relations, events, time), semantic role labeling, lexicons for sentiment/affect/connotation, coreference resolution and entity linking, discourse coherence, and conversation structure.
