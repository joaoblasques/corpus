# Speech and Language Processing, 3rd Edition Draft (Jurafsky & Martin, 2026)

TL;DR: The definitive NLP and computational linguistics textbook by Daniel Jurafsky (Stanford) and James H. Martin (University of Colorado). Third edition draft (January 6, 2026; 626 pages); covers LLMs, transformers, NLP pipelines, speech recognition, and linguistic structure annotation. Freely available online. See [Daniel Jurafsky](/ai-engineering/daniel-jurafsky.md) and [James H. Martin](/ai-engineering/james-martin.md).

## Key ideas

- **Tokenization and BPE** (Chapter 2): ELIZA's pattern matching was the precursor to modern tokenization; byte-pair encoding (BPE) iteratively merges the most frequent adjacent byte pairs — enables subword tokenization handling OOV words without a fixed vocabulary. [^slp-p01]
- **N-gram smoothing** (Chapter 3): add-one smoothing drastically reallocates mass — bigram `want to` went from P=0.66 to 0.26 (discount d=0.39), `Chinese food` discount d=0.10; Kneser-Ney is the standard for production n-gram LMs. [^slp-p05]
- **Cosine similarity for embeddings** (Chapter 5): raw dot product biases toward frequent (longer) word vectors; normalizing by vector L2-norms gives the cosine — a frequency-invariant semantic similarity measure used throughout NLP. [^slp-p08]
- **Transformer architecture** (Chapter 8): each token is first embedded via matrix E then passed through N transformer blocks; the residual stream adds attention and FFN contributions at each layer; positional encoding is added to the embedding (not a separate input). [^slp-p12]
- **Inverted index** (Chapter 11): consists of a dictionary (each term with document frequency + pointer) and postings lists (document IDs + term counts); enables efficient TF-IDF ranking; stop words are less commonly used now since IDF naturally downweights function words. [^slp-p16]
- **Machine Translation typology** (Chapter 12): languages vary in verb-framed vs satellite-framed motion encoding (Talmy 1985), morphological typology (isolating/agglutinative/fusion), pro-drop, and referential density — all sources of translation difficulty; lexical gaps require explanatory paraphrase. [^slp-p17]
- **MT evaluation metrics** (Chapter 12): chrF (character F-score) is preferred over BLEU for morphologically rich languages; BERTSCORE uses cosine similarity of BERT token embeddings for greedy precision/recall; COMET/BLEURT fine-tune BERT on human MT quality ratings. [^slp-p18]
- **Bidirectional RNNs and LSTMs** (Chapter 13): a bidirectional RNN concatenates forward and backward hidden states at each position, giving left+right context; LSTMs (Chapter 13) add a cell state with explicit forget/input/output gates to handle long-range dependencies that vanilla RNNs lose via gradient vanishing. [^slp-p19]
- **Phonetics and speech features** (Chapter 14): place of articulation (bilabial, alveolar, velar, glottal) × manner (stop, fricative, approximant, nasal) characterizes consonants; vowels by height × frontness × rounding; log-mel features extracted via 25ms windows with 10ms stride; PCM quantization maps continuous amplitudes to 8/16-bit integers. [^slp-p20]
- **Whisper ASR architecture** (Chapter 15): Whisper uses two convolutional layers (stride 1 then stride 2) to reduce 3000 input audio frames to 1500 audio tokens (20ms each), then a transformer encoder + cross-attention decoder; the decoder generates text tokens conditioned on encoder audio representations — exactly the encoder-decoder MT architecture. [^slp-p22]
- **Zero-shot TTS** (Chapter 16): modern TTS trains on tens of thousands of speakers; a codec tokenizer converts speech into discrete audio tokens; a language model conditioned on text tokens + a 3-second voice sample generates audio tokens in the target voice — never seen in training. [^slp-p23]
- **POS tagging and CRF** (Chapter 17): HMM taggers maximize P(tag|word) × P(tag|prev-tag) via Viterbi; CRF (discriminative) conditions on the full input sequence via log-linear features; morphologically rich languages (Turkish, Czech) need per-word morphological analysis with 4–10× larger tagsets. [^slp-p25]
- **CKY constituency parsing** (Chapter 18): requires CNF grammar (all rules binary or unit); fills an n×n table bottom-up; O(n³ × |R|) dynamic programming; the table accumulates all alternative parses for a span, enabling extraction of the best via probabilistic PCFG scoring. [^slp-p26]
- **Dependency parsing — arc-eager** (Chapter 19): transition-based parser with a stack and buffer; LEFTARC/RIGHTARC assert dependency relations, SHIFT moves to buffer; the oracle selects transitions according to a reference answer, deferring arcs until all dependents are attached. [^slp-p27]
- **Coreference resolution** (Chapter 23): mention detection extracts NPs, possessive pronouns, and named entities; span-based systems propose all n-gram spans up to length 10 then filter; WinoBias and GAP benchmarks reveal systematic gender bias (≥21 F1-point gap between pro- and anti-stereotypical pronoun links across all architectures). [^slp-p32]
- **Coverage note**: all 38 parts ingested (complete textbook). Parts 35-38 are bibliography.
