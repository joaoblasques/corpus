# Data-Driven Science and Engineering (Brunton & Kutz, 2021)

TL;DR: Comprehensive textbook on data-driven methods for dynamical systems and engineering — bridges classical signal processing (SVD, FFT, wavelets), modern machine learning (neural networks, classification), and control theory (LQR, MPC, reinforcement learning) via a unified framework of sparse representations and data-driven dynamics discovery.

## Overview

Authors: Steven L. Brunton (Mechanical Engineering, University of Washington) and J. Nathan Kutz (Applied Mathematics, University of Washington). Published by Cambridge University Press, 2021. 761 pages. An author-free edition is publicly available.

The book's central thesis: many physical systems have low-dimensional structure hidden in high-dimensional data. The goal of data-driven science is to discover and exploit this structure using mathematical tools from linear algebra, signal processing, optimization, and machine learning [^src1].

## Structure

The book is organized into four parts:

| Part | Topic | Key methods |
|---|---|---|
| I | Dimensionality Reduction & Transforms | SVD, PCA, FFT, wavelets, compressed sensing |
| II | Machine Learning & Data Analysis | Regression, classification, clustering, neural networks |
| III | Dynamical Systems | DMD, SINDy, Koopman operators, network dynamics |
| IV | Control | LQR, LQG, Kalman filter, MPC, reinforcement learning |

## Key methods covered

- [Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)](/ai-engineering/singular-value-decomposition.md) — optimal low-rank matrix approximation; foundation for PCA, DMD, and reduced-order models
- [Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD)](/ai-engineering/dynamic-mode-decomposition.md) — spectral decomposition of nonlinear dynamical systems from data; connects to Koopman operator theory
- [SINDy](/ai-engineering/sindy.md) — sparse identification of governing equations from time-series data using ℓ₁ optimization
- [Compressed Sensing](/ai-engineering/compressed-sensing.md) — recovery of sparse signals from far fewer measurements than Shannon-Nyquist requires
- [Koopman Operator](/ai-engineering/koopman-operator.md) — infinite-dimensional linear operator that lifts nonlinear dynamics to a linear (observable) space

## Relevance to AI engineering

While primarily a scientific computing textbook, DDSE is foundational for:
- Understanding the mathematical substrate of modern ML (PCA, regression, neural nets)
- Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs)
- Operator learning (a precursor to neural operators like FNO)
- Reduced-order modeling (ROMs) using SVD + neural networks for time-stepping

[^src1]: Data-Driven Science and Engineering, Brunton & Kutz, Cambridge University Press, 2021 (parts 1–31 as PDF extracts)
