# History of Money

TL;DR: Money evolved from barter through collectibles, metals, coinage, paper, and fiat — each stage increasing abstraction and social trust. Bitcoin (2009) is a direct ideological response to the 2008 banking crisis and represents another step in that evolution: trustless, decentralized digital money.

## The problem with barter

Barter fails at scale due to the "double coincidence of wants" — both parties must each want exactly what the other offers simultaneously [^src1]. This made complex economies impossible without a medium of exchange.

## Three properties of money (Jevons, 1875)

Every successful form of money satisfies three requirements [^src1]:

1. **Store of value** — holds purchasing power over time
2. **Medium of exchange** — accepted in trade for goods/services
3. **Unit of account** — prices can be denominated in it

## Evolution timeline

| Era | Form | Key event |
|---|---|---|
| Prehistoric | Collectibles (shells, beads) | Small, durable, hard to forge, easy to appraise |
| ~1000 BC | Precious metals | Durability + divisibility |
| ~600 BC | Coinage | Stamped authentication; began in Turkey |
| 1000 AD | Fiat paper money | China's Song Dynasty — first unbacked currency |
| 1913 | Central banking | US Federal Reserve founded |
| 1944–1971 | Bretton Woods | USD backed by gold; anchor for other currencies |
| 1971 | Pure fiat | Nixon ended gold convertibility — USD floats freely |
| 1950s–2002 | Card infrastructure | Diner's Club → Visa → PayPal (built on card rails) |
| 2009 | Bitcoin | Trustless decentralized digital money |

## Money as social consensus

The Yapese people of Yap used enormous limestone Rai stones as money. One stone that sank to the ocean floor was still considered valid currency because community members remembered and accepted the ownership record [^src1]. This illustrates a key principle: "stable social consensus about its value" is the only requirement for money — not intrinsic worth or physical possession.

## Bitcoin's ideological origin

Bitcoin's Genesis Block embedded a 2009 Times headline about bank bailouts as a deliberate statement [^src1]. Bitcoin was designed as trustless infrastructure that removes the need for central bank trust entirely — a direct response to the erosion of trust exposed by the 2008 financial crisis.

Bitcoin's first recorded price: $0.003/coin (2010). First commercial transaction: 10,000 BTC for two pizzas [^src1].
