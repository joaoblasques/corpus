Most operators and solopreneurs have a strong offer. They just cannot explain it clearly.

That gap lives in the copy. The website headline. The sales email. The ad. The thing they say on a call when someone asks what they do. All of it drifts because no one has ever written the offer down in one clean document.

------------------------------------------------------------
The Play - Build Your AI Offer Positioning Doc in 20 Minutes
------------------------------------------------------------

‚Äã
‚Äã

The problem: Operators are writing ads, emails, and sales pages off an offer they have never fully articulated. The result is marketing that sounds like everyone else in the market.

‚Äã
‚Äã

The setup: One Cowork skill that interviews you about your offer and outputs a positioning one-pager you can use to brief copywriters, write ads, and prep every sales call.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Tools needed:‚Äã
‚Äã

* Claude Cowork desktop app
* 20 uninterrupted minutes
* Perplexity (optional but useful for ICP language research beforehand, covered below)

‚Äã
‚Äã

Step 1: Build the interviewer skill in Cowork.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Open Cowork and type: "Create a skill called offer-positioning." When the skill editor opens, paste this prompt:

‚Äã
‚Äã

"You are a direct response positioning strategist. Your job is to interview me about my offer and extract the clearest, most compelling version of my positioning. Ask me one question at a time. Wait for my answer before asking the next one. Do not summarize until I have answered all questions. Here are the questions: (1) What is your offer and what does it do in one sentence? (2) Who is it specifically for, and who is it not for? (3) What is the outcome a buyer gets, and by when? (4) What are the three biggest reasons someone would not buy this, and what is your response to each? (5) What proof do you have that it works? Name specific results, case studies, or client outcomes. (6) What makes this different from every other option your buyer could choose? (7) What is the one thing your ideal buyer is most afraid of when they consider buying? When I have answered all seven questions, output a positioning one-pager in this format: Headline, Ideal Buyer, The Problem, The Offer, Proof Points (3), Primary Objection and Reframe, Differentiator."

‚Äã
‚Äã

Step 2: Run the interview.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Open the skill and let it ask you questions one at a time. Answer each one directly. Do not overthink it. The first answer that comes to mind is usually the truest version of what you actually believe. The whole interview takes 15 to 20 minutes.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Step 3: Tighten the output.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Read the positioning one-pager it produces. The headline is often the weakest section on the first pass. If it sounds generic, ask Cowork: "Rewrite the headline to lead with the specific outcome and the specific buyer. No buzzwords." Repeat until a stranger could read it and immediately know whether the offer is for them or not.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Step 4: Put it to work.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Save the doc somewhere your whole operation can access it. Paste it into every AI prompt you write for ads, emails, and landing pages as context. Share it with your copywriter as a brief. Read it before sales calls. This one document becomes the source of truth for how your offer gets talked about everywhere it shows up.

‚Äã
‚Äã

The result: One operator who ran this version said it was the first time he had seen his offer written down in a way that made him want to buy it himself. He used the output to rewrite his website headline, brief his email writer, and prep his sales script. He closed 11 out of 14 calls on the next launch.

‚Äã
‚Äã

Make it yours: Add an eighth question asking about your buyer's emotional state at the moment they find you. What are they feeling? What have they already tried? That answer usually surfaces the strongest headline you have never written. If you have multiple offers or audiences, run the skill once per offer. The outputs stack into a positioning library that improves every piece of marketing you create.
