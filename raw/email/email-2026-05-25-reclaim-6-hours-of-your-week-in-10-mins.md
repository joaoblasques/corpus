Happy Monday. It’s about 9 am, your coffee’s hot, and your brain hasn’t been hijacked by Slack yet. This is the most valuable 30 minutes of your week, so let’s not waste it answering emails. Let’s set up the machine that’ll answer them for you.
Here is how you can streamline your entire week with claude
The problem: Most people open a blank Claude chat every single morning and re-explain who they are. That’s like onboarding a new employee daily and firing them at 5pm. Exhausting, and you never get the good stuff.
Spend 15 minutes once this morning, and every check-in for the rest of the week takes 30 seconds. Here’s the ritual.
Step 1: Stop the bread-crumbing:
By default, Claude opens with "Great question! Let me break this down for you…" — three lines you didn't ask for, every time and it costs you credits.
Newer Claude models follow instructions very literally, which is a gift: tell it exactly how to behave and it sticks. Go to Settings → Profile preferences (free on every plan) and paste something like this:
Prompt:
“ Skip the preamble. No “great question,” no restating what I asked, no closing summary unless I ask. Lead with the answer directly.

When I ask for options, give me your actual recommendation, not a neutral list.

Be direct. If I’m about to do something dumb, say so.

Before you start, ask me the 5 most important questions that would help you do this well.

After I answer, then begin. "
Two rules from Anthropic’s own playbook [ https://substack.com/redirect/8996dbfe-8478-40b6-8897-21919cd0432e?j=eyJ1IjoibjBrMyJ9.SQ5ZKHxJh0MocVfrsArKZFhT_ts26OEfWqtevr5N8ms ] make this stick: say what to do, not what to avoid (”lead with the answer” beats “don’t ramble”), and explain the why when it matters (”be concise, I read these on my phone”). The model generalises from reasons.
Step 2: Build a Project That Writes Like You:
Think of a Project as a workspace with a memory , your instructions and files ride along into every chat inside it. Build 2-3 you'll open daily, and feed each one your style guide, past issues, and brand voice. Here's mine.
Project: “Newsletter.” Train it on your voice with 3–5 real samples (Anthropic says 3–5 examples [ https://substack.com/redirect/8996dbfe-8478-40b6-8897-21919cd0432e?j=eyJ1IjoibjBrMyJ9.SQ5ZKHxJh0MocVfrsArKZFhT_ts26OEfWqtevr5N8ms ] is the sweet spot for steering tone).
Prompt:
" Here are 4 things I’ve written: [paste]. Don’t describe my “vibe” — extract the patterns: sentence length, how I open and close, words I reach for, what I never do. From now on, draft in this style and don’t drift back to your defaults. And don’t summarise the write up unless I ask you to. "
Now, all week: “draft Thursday’s intro” → it sounds like you, not a press release.
Step 3: Before you manager, make Claude question you:
After being in the ecosystem for so long I have realised that underspecified chatter actually lowers answer quality. So flip it: Ask it not just the questions that confuse you but also the ones that should or already question you . 
Prompt:
"Here’s my plan: Before you give your opinion on any of my ideas use this flow

Step 1: Attack it. Find every assumption that could be wrong and every realistic way it fails. Don't be polite.

Step 2: Steel-man it. Make the strongest possible case that I'm right.

Step 3: Tell me what you actually think, and what you'd change after considering both the ways it could go very wrong and slightly right."
Two minutes answering > twenty minutes correcting. Every time.
The 30% Freebie:
When you paste a long doc or dataset, put it at the top and your question at the bottom. Anthropic’s testing found query-last ordering can improve answer quality by up to 30% [ https://substack.com/redirect/8996dbfe-8478-40b6-8897-21919cd0432e?j=eyJ1IjoibjBrMyJ9.SQ5ZKHxJh0MocVfrsArKZFhT_ts26OEfWqtevr5N8ms ] on big inputs.

Two Minute Papers [ https://substack.com/redirect/2997bcfe-ddfc-4a95-9255-7eb80921b050?j=eyJ1IjoibjBrMyJ9.SQ5ZKHxJh0MocVfrsArKZFhT_ts26OEfWqtevr5N8ms ]  — bite-sized, genuinely delightful breakdowns of the latest AI research, delivered with the most infectious “hold on to your papers!” energy on the internet.

Context as infrastructure, taste as config, scale via delegation.
