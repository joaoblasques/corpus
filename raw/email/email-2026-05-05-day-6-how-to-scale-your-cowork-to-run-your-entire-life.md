Hi Joao,

Today’s email is all about how you scale your Cowork OS folder
over the next week, next month…and even maybe the next decade! 🔮

By the end of this email: You'll understand the three mechanisms
that make a 3-month-old workspace dramatically better than a
3-day-old one, and you'll install a skill that starts the
self-improvement cycle from your very next session.

-------------------
Accumulating Memory
-------------------

Every session, Cowork can learn something new about how you work:
a decision, a preference, a correction. With the memory system
from Day 1, these get written to the appropriate MEMORY.md and
persist across sessions.

Your voice principles, one week from now
----------------------------------------

You ask Cowork to draft an email:

* It uses "Best regards" but you always sign off with "Thanks."
* You correct it.
* Cowork recognizes that this correction isn't captured in your
voice-principles.md yet, and asks: "Should I add this?"
* You say yes.
* Cowork opens the file, adds the rule, saves it. Every email
draft from that point forward uses your sign-off. You'll never
correct it again.

A month in, you've made ten corrections like this. Cowork knows
you never use exclamation points in subject lines, that you write
"e.g." not "for example," that you start replies with the
person's name, never "Hi there." Each correction took 10 seconds.
The cumulative result is an AI that writes like you without being
told.

​
The same pattern plays out across every file in your workspace.

* Your Email HQ contacts CRM grows from 10 entries to 50+ as
Cowork logs every new person you email, how you talk to them, and
what topics come up.
* Your project MEMORY.md accumulates every decision you make, so
next month's statement import or savings check-in builds on
everything that came before it.

One memory entry per session doesn't sound like much. But after
90 sessions, you have 90 contextual facts Cowork applies
automatically. That's the difference between a tool that does
what you say and an assistant that knows what you mean.

-------
Routing
-------

In Day 4, you added a routing table to your root CLAUDE.md with
two rows: email and personal finances.

Here's what happens when you add a third workstation. You tell
Cowork: "Create a Travel workstation for trip planning." Cowork
generates the folder, and adds a new row to your routing table.
Three rows.

Next time you mention a flight or a hotel, Cowork loads Travel.
It doesn't touch Email HQ or Personal Finances.

Why this matters more as you scale. Without routing, Cowork loads
everything every session: your voice principles, your email
rules, your financial data, your subscription audit notes
(whether it needs them or not).

Every new workstation you add without routing makes every session
slower. Every new workstation you add with routing stays
invisible until it's needed.

My routing table has a LOT of rows. Each row is one sentence.
That's one of the highest-leverage things in my entire workspace,
because Cowork loads only what it needs, every session,
automatically.

--------------
Session Audits
--------------

You can teach Cowork to check its own work. At the end of a
session, you say "audit this session." Cowork scans everything
that happened and proposes changes: rules to add to CLAUDE.md,
context to save to MEMORY.md, things it should do differently
next time.

In plain English: Cowork reviews its own homework before you
close out. When it finds something worth remembering, it asks
your permission to save it.

​
For example: You drafted two emails and reviewed your
subscription audit, then you say "audit this session." Cowork
comes back with:

* "You corrected the sign-off on your vendor email, but your
Email HQ rules don't have a vendor convention yet. Should I add
one?"

You approve. Cowork writes it. Tomorrow it follows that rule
without being told.

After 30 sessions: you start noticing that Cowork almost never
needs correcting. Every audit made the rules more precise, the
memory more complete, and the outputs more aligned with how you
actually work.
