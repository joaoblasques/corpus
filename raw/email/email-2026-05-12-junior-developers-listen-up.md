Hey Joao,

If learning to code feels harder than you expected, that does not
mean you are doing it wrong.

It simply means you are doing something that is actually hard.

One of the worst parts of programming is that you can spend hours
stuck on one bug, one setup issue, or one tiny problem that
should feel simple, but somehow is not.

That kind of frustration is normal.

In fact, even experienced developers still deal with it.

You can spend half a day stuck, go to sleep annoyed, wake up, try
again, and only then figure it out.

That is part of the process.

But over time, everything will change.

You stop seeing every hard problem as proof that you are not good
enough.

Instead, it is simply something that you have to work through.

Because once you have been stuck enough times and still found
your way through, you build a kind of confidence that is hard to
get any other way.

You know that every problem can be solved.

And with that said, everyone’s path into coding looks different.

That is why comparing yourself to other people is such a bad
idea.

The internet is full of stories about people who learned in a few
months, got a great job at 19, or built something huge right
away.

But that is not how it goes for everyone.

People learn at a completely different pace. Some move fast. Some
take longer. But that does not decide how good you can become.

What matters is whether you keep going.

And if you do, software development can become one of the most
valuable skills you ever build.

It can give you more freedom over where you work, how you work,
and what kind of opportunities you can create for yourself.

So if you have been feeling stuck lately, do not take that as a
sign to quit.

Take it as a sign that you are learning something difficult.

Keep going,

Tim
