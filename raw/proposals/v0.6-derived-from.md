# v0.6 Proposal: `derived_from:` field

**Status:** draft
**Motivating page:** `distributed-systems-fallacies.md`
**Date:** 2026-05-22

## Problem

Synthesis pages currently express upstream corpus-internal dependencies only in prose. The `sources:` field captures *external* citations (books, papers, talks) but conflates them with *internal* provenance — the fact that a synthesis page stands on top of other corpus pages.

Consequences:
- No structured provenance graph for internal knowledge.
- No way to detect downstream impact when a foundational page changes.
- Synthesis pages can only be identified by reading prose.
- The relationship "page A builds on page B" has no first-class representation.

## Proposal

Add an optional frontmatter field `derived_from:` listing canonical corpus slugs that the current page synthesizes or builds upon.

```yaml
---
title: Eight Fallacies of Distributed Computing
status: draft
sources:
  - deutsch-fallacies-1994
  - rotem-gal-oz-fallacies-explained
  - gregor-hohpe-talk
derived_from:
  - network-reliability
  - latency-vs-bandwidth
  - cap-theorem
  - eventual-consistency
---
```

## Semantics

- **Target type:** canonical corpus page slug. Same identifier shape used for internal links.
- **Cardinality:** list, 0..n. Field omitted when empty (not `[]`).
- **Direction:** upstream-only. Page A's field lists pages A depends on. The reverse index ("what depends on A") is computed, never stored.
- **Relationship to `sources:`:** orthogonal, both can be present. `sources:` = external citations. `derived_from:` = internal page provenance. A synthesis page typically has both. `derived_from:` is never a substitute for `sources:`.
- **Synthesis-page detection:** presence of non-empty `derived_from:` marks a page as a synthesis page. No separate `kind:` or `type:` field needed for v0.6.

## Validation rules (lint)

1. **Existence:** every slug in `derived_from:` must resolve to an existing corpus page. Missing target = lint error.
2. **Acyclicity:** derivation forms a DAG. Cycles rejected. Lint walks the graph.
3. **No self-reference:** a page cannot list itself.
4. **No source substitution:** `derived_from:` does not satisfy the `sources:` requirement. Graduation rules (≥3 sources) operate on `sources:` only.
5. **Warn at cardinality 1:** a single-entry `derived_from:` is suspicious — likely belongs as a related-page link or inline reference. Warn, don't error.

## Migration

- v0.5 pages remain valid v0.6 pages. Additive change, no breaking modifications.
- No backfill of existing pages in this proposal. Retroactive review is a later phase.
- `distributed-systems-fallacies.md` is the first target for `derived_from:` once v0.6 lands.

## Open questions

1. **Slug vs path as target identifier.**
   Recommendation: bare slug. Slug uniqueness is already a v0.5 invariant; paths add noise.

2. **Minimum cardinality for "synthesis".**
   Recommendation: no hard floor. Warn at 1. A page with 1 entry is probably mis-classified.

3. **Transitive closure surfacing.**
   Should lint or tooling show "effective ancestors" (transitive `derived_from:`)?
   Recommendation: defer to tooling, not schema. v0.6 stores the direct relationship only.

4. **Interaction with v0.6 §9 collision-rule cleanup.**
   Both touch the page-to-page relationship surface. Address as a separate sub-item in the v0.6 cycle, not within this proposal.

## Non-goals for v0.6

- No `derives_to:` reverse field — always computed, never stored.
- No automatic graduation propagation through derivation chains. Source-count inheritance is a v0.7 question.
- No graph visualization tooling. Separate work item.
- No retroactive backfill of existing pages. Separate phase after spec lands.

## Phasing

- **Phase 1 (current):** this design doc in `raw/proposals/`. No edits to corpus pages or v0.5 spec.
- **Phase 2:** lint rules drafted and tested against current corpus snapshot. Diff-then-apply.
- **Phase 3:** v0.6 spec written, exemplar (`distributed-systems-fallacies.md`) populated.
- **Phase 4:** retroactive review of synthesis-adjacent pages.

## Risks

- **Over-eager tagging:** authors may add `derived_from:` to express loose relatedness rather than true derivation. Mitigated by the cardinality-1 warning and clear spec language.
- **Slug rename fragility:** if a target slug is renamed, `derived_from:` references break. Lint catches this. Out-of-scope: domain-rename-as-first-class is separately queued for v0.6.
- **Scope creep into v0.7:** transitive closure, graduation inheritance, reverse indexes — all tempting, all deferred.
